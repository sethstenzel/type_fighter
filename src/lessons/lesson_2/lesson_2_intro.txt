Welcome back, pilot ... to the typefighter training program.

You survived your first simulation, and you learned your keyboard launch position:
the home row.

Last time, your focus was on the F and J keys. Those were your navigation beacons.
Your pointer fingers used them to find the home row without looking down.

Today, we are expanding your control panel.

Your new mission keys are D, K, and ␣.

The D key belongs to your left middle finger.
The K key belongs to your right middle finger.
The ␣ belongs to your thumbs. Use whichever thumb feels natural and steady.

That means your left middle finger should rest on D.
And your right middle finger should rest on K.

Do not move your whole hand to reach them.
Do not look down to find them.
Your hands should already be sitting on the home row.

On your left hand:
Your pinky rests on A.
Your ring finger rests on S.
Your middle finger rests on D.
Your pointer finger rests on F.

On your right hand:
Your pointer finger rests on J.
Your middle finger rests on K.
Your ring finger rests on L.
Your pinky rests on the semicolon key.

Now check your formation.

Left middle finger on D.
Right middle finger on K.
Either thumb ready over the ␣.

Feel where your pointer fingers are on F and J.
Use those raised bumps to confirm your hands are still locked into the home row.

That is important, pilot.

Your middle fingers should move only when it is time to press D or K.
After pressing the key, they return to their home row positions.

Press D with your left middle finger.
Then return that finger to D.

Press K with your right middle finger.
Then return that finger to K.

Press ␣ with either thumb.
Then let that thumb float back to ready position.

This is how real typing control is built:
small movements, steady hands, eyes forward.

If you look down, you break your scan.
If you move your whole hand, you lose your launch position.
If you trust the home row, your fingers will know where to go.

In this mission, enemy drones will carry D, K, or ␣.
When you see D, press D with your left middle finger.
When you see K, press K with your right middle finger.
When you see ␣, press the ␣ with either thumb.

Keep your pointer fingers near F and J.
Keep your middle fingers ready on D and K.
Keep one thumb ready for ␣.
Keep your eyes on the screen.

Before you launch, place both hands on the home row.
Make sure your left middle finger is resting on D.
Make sure your right middle finger is resting on K.
Make sure either thumb can reach the ␣ without moving your whole hand.

Once your hands are locked into position, it is time for eyes forward.

No looking down until the lesson is complete.

To launch the training simulation, use either thumb to press the ␣.
