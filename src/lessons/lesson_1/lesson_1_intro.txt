Welcome pilot ... to the Type Fighter training program.

Today marks the beginning... of your official Type Fighter Training.

Before we throw you into real combat, we need to make sure you can handle your keyboard.

I'm going to start with the basics...

That means learning the home row and the two most important keys on the home row... 'F' and 'J'...

The home row is the middle row of your keyboard.
Your home row launch position starts on the left side with the the A Key, and ends on the right side the semicolon.

Your keyboard launch position is where your fingers rest between keypresses, and where your fingers should always return after flying out to press other keys.

To start...

On your left hand...
Put your left pinky on the 'A' key...
Put your left ring finger on the 'S' key...
Put your left middle finger on the 'D' key...
Put your left pointer finger on the 'F' key...

And on your right hand...
Put your right pointer finger on the 'J' key.
Put your right middle finger on the 'K' key.
Put your right ring finger on the 'L' key.
And finally put your right pinky on the semicolon key.

Your hands should now be on the home row...

Now, pilot, pay very close attention...

The 'F' and 'J' keys are your navigation beacons.
They each have a tiny raised bump on them. And those little bumps help your pointer fingers find the home row without looking down.

That means even in total darkness, your fingers can still find their way back to the home row launch position.

Now listen carefully pilot, as what I have to say next is vital to your career here.
The most important thing if you want to become 'A' real Type Fighter Pilot:

Never.. and I mean never ever... look at the keyboard while defending if you can help it. It's the biggest rule here at Type Fighter School.

Looking down breaks your focus... takes your eyes off the enemies... slows your speed... and makes your fingers lazy.
'A' real Type Fighter Pilot learns to trust their navigation beacons with those tiny raised bumps... build their key position memory... and to trust their training.

So before you begin your first training mission, take one quick look at the keyboard. Place your fingers on the home row.
Make sure your pointer fingers can feel those little bumps on 'F' and 'J'...

Once your hands are locked into position, it's time for eyes forward.

No more looking down until the lesson is complete... You’ve got this, rookie.

To launch the training simulation, place both hands on the home row. Then use either thumb to press the Space Bar key — that big long key at the bottom of the keyboard.

Good Luck Pilot...
